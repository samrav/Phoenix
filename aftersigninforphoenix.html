Success Stories

People who have successfully reentered society.

  • Glenn Martin

    For some people all it takes is one event to discover their life’s mission. That moment came for Glenn Martin after he served six years in New York state prison for armed robbery and embarked upon empowerment of those involved with the criminal justice system. That journey has led to his present role as the vice president of development and public affairs and director of the David Rothenberg Center for Public Policy (DRCPP) at The Fortune Society, Inc., a non-profit dedicated to the reintegration of the formerly incarcerated. Martin has also built a reputation as one of the leading experts on criminal justice after serving as project manager on the largest audit ever conducted in the U.S. on discrimination in low-wage labor markets. Martin can be found spreading the word about his mission in the June 2011 issue of Ebony magazine.

  • Susan Burton

    It takes strength, courage and perseverance to overcome substance abuse. Perhaps even more impressively, it takes a rare form of compassion to then help carry the burdens of other people’s addictions. Susan Burton possesses that rare trait. After nearly fifteen years in-and-out of the criminal justice system she founded A New Way of Life Reentry Project in 1998. To date the California based organization has assisted over 500 formerly incarcerated women by offering shelter, safety, leadership and support. In 2010 CNN recognized Burton’s accomplishments naming her a Top 10 Hero. She is presently working on a screenplay of her inspirational story.

  • Christopher S. Linder

    For the formerly incarcerated reentry into society is often about reinvention. It was less than two years ago that Christopher S. Linder’s parole ended for crimes related to gang activity and drug dealing. Now he stands as the mayor-elect for the small town of Pawnee, Oklahoma. However, despite managing to win support of over forty percent of the town’s majority white voters, Oklahoma law prevents Linder from taking office until his record is wiped clean . Even though Linder’s request to take office has been denied he’s still making a positive contribution to his community as the co-owner of the Pawnee Café, volunteer board member of the local Chamber of Commerce and baseball coach.

  • Tina Reynolds

    The passion for advocacy work is sometimes best derived from personal experience. As the co-founder and chair of Women on the Rise Telling HerStory (WORTH), one of the main issues that Reynolds has focused on is the shackling of pregnant women during childbirth, something she experienced during her time of incarceration. In 2009 WORTH and the Correctional Association of New York succeeded in persuading former Governor David Patterson to enact the Anti-Shackling Bill. She is currently an adjunct professor at York College in the Psychology Department and is an editor of the anthology, Interrupted Life: Experiences of Incarcerated Women in the United States. Reynolds’ passion for reform continues to challenge policies that impose high barriers for women in the criminal justice system.
